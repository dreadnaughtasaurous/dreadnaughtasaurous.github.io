# How to Search

This wiki has two ways to find information:

1. **Search** — fast keyword search powered by **Pagefind**, running directly in your browser.
2. **Ask AI** — an AI-assisted search mode that reads wiki content and answers a question using pages from the wiki itself.

The Search modal is designed to help you either:
- find the exact clause page quickly, or
- ask a plain-language question and be guided to the relevant source pages.

## Opening Search

Click the **Search** bar in the top navigation, or use a keyboard shortcut:

| Action | Shortcut |
|---|---|
| Open search | `Ctrl` + `K` (Windows) or `/` |
| Navigate results | `↑` `↓` arrow keys |
| Open a highlighted result | `Enter` |
| Close search | `Esc` |

When the modal opens, you can switch between the **Search** tab and the **Ask AI** tab.

## Search Tab

The **Search** tab is the standard keyword search. It is best when you want to:

- find a specific clause number
- search for a known term such as `overtime`, `allowance`, or `parental leave`
- narrow results by EBA or topic
- browse likely pages quickly

Type a word or phrase into the search box. Results are ranked by relevance and may show:

- the **clause title**
- the **EBA** the page belongs to
- a **section / clause breadcrumb**
- a **highlighted excerpt**
- any matching **topic tags**

Every clause page also carries search synonyms alongside formal EBA terminology, so everyday language can still lead you to the right page.

| If you search for… | You may find… |
|---|---|
| `sick day`, `off crook` | Personal Leave |
| `time off for a funeral` | Compassionate Leave / Ceremonial Leave |
| `after hours phone calls` | Right to Disconnect |
| `called back to work` | Recall to Duty / Overtime |
| `free meals`, `fed at work` | Meal Allowance |
| `weekend penalty` | Penalty Rates / Saturday / Sunday loadings |
| `study leave`, `course fees` | Professional Development / Education Leave |
| `extra pay for qualifications` | Higher Qualifications Allowance / Top of Band |

### Filters

Below the search box, the Search tab includes two filters:

#### Filter by EBA

Use the **EBA** dropdown to restrict results to one agreement only.

#### Filter by Topic

Use the **Topic** dropdown when you know the subject area but not the clause number.

This is especially useful for broad topics such as:

`allowances` · `leave` · `overtime` · `penalty-rates` · `wages` · `termination` · `workload`

#### Using both together

You can combine:
- a keyword
- an EBA filter
- a Topic filter

A page must match the active search conditions to appear in the results.

::: tip Example
To find wage-related pages only for Nurses and Midwives, open Search, choose the **Nurses and Midwives 2024–2028** EBA, then select the **wages** topic or use the **Wage Rates** quick access shortcut.
:::

To remove restrictions, click **Clear filters**.

### Quick Access

When the Search tab opens with no active query and no filters, a **Quick access** panel is shown.

This gives one-click shortcuts into common topics, including:

- **Overtime & Penalty Rates**
- **Leave Entitlements**
- **Wage Rates**
- **Allowances**
- **Termination & Redundancy**

Clicking one of these shortcuts automatically applies a topic filter and runs the search for that topic.

For example, clicking **Wage Rates** filters results to pages tagged with the `wages` topic.

### Recent Searches

The Search tab can also show a **Recent searches** section.

This lets you quickly re-run a recent query without typing it again. If needed, you can clear the recent search history from within the modal.

### Result Ranking and Relevance

Search results are not simply shown in alphabetical order. They are ranked so the most relevant pages appear first.

The wiki search has been improved so that pages more closely aligned to the searched topic are surfaced more effectively, helping the most useful clause pages appear earlier in the results list.

In practice, this means:
- exact or close topic matches tend to rank higher
- pages strongly associated with a selected topic are prioritised
- filters help reduce noise when many clauses mention similar words

### Desktop Preview Pane

On a **desktop browser**, hovering over a search result shows a **preview pane** to the right of the search modal.

This preview helps you inspect a result before opening it. It may show:

- the page title
- the EBA
- the section or clause breadcrumb
- an excerpt
- topic tags

The preview pane is intended for larger screens only, so it may not appear on smaller screens or mobile devices.

### No Results and Fuzzy Matching

If your search returns no direct matches, the search may try to show results for a **similar term** instead.

This helps when:
- a word is slightly misspelled
- you use a close variant of the actual indexed term
- the clause uses different wording than you expected

If no useful keyword results can be found, the modal may suggest switching to the **Ask AI** tab for a direct answer.

## Ask AI Tab

The **Ask AI** tab is for natural-language questions.

Use it when you want to ask something like:

- “Is a full-time employee under the Nurses and Midwives EBA entitled to overtime on a public holiday?”
- “How much notice is required before changing the roster of a part-time nurse?”
- “What is the recall allowance for a Grade 3 Allied Health employee?”

Instead of just listing matching pages, Ask AI reads wiki content and returns a written answer based on the wiki itself.

This is most useful when:
- you are unsure which clause to open
- the issue spans multiple clauses
- you want a plain-language starting point before reading the full clause text

### Sources Used

After answering, the Ask AI tab shows a **Sources used** section.

Each source is a hyperlink to the corresponding wiki page, so you can immediately open the clauses the answer relied on.

This is important because AI answers are meant to help you locate and understand relevant material faster — the linked source pages remain the authoritative pages to read in full.

### Best Way to Use Ask AI

Ask AI works best when your question is specific.

Try to include:
- the **EBA**
- the **employee type / classification** if relevant
- the **entitlement or issue**
- the **circumstance**, such as overtime, public holiday work, recall, or roster change

For example:

- Better: `Is a full-time employee under the Nurses and Midwives EBA entitled to overtime pay on a public holiday?`
- Less useful: `Do I get paid more?`

The more precise your question, the more likely the answer and source list will point you to the right clauses.

### Important Note About Ask AI

Ask AI is a research aid, not a substitute for reading the clause itself.

AI answers are generated from wiki content only, and you should always verify the answer against the linked source pages and the full EBA text before relying on it.

## Tips for Better Results

- **Use Search** when you know the term, clause number, or topic.
- **Use Ask AI** when you want a plain-language answer to a question.
- **Use fewer words** in keyword search, for example `annual leave cashing out` rather than a full sentence.
- **Use filters early** if you already know the EBA or subject area.
- **Try synonyms** if your first keyword does not work.
- **Use Quick access** for common categories such as wages, leave, overtime, and allowances.
- **Preview before opening** on desktop if several results look similar.
- **Check the source links** after using Ask AI.

## Archive Search

The default search does **not** include superseded or expired Enterprise Agreements.

If you need to look up historical entitlements — for example, to interpret something that occurred under a previous agreement — use the dedicated [Archive Search](/eba/archive/search/).
