# How to Search

This wiki has two ways to find information:

1. **Search** — fast keyword search powered by Pagefind, running directly in your browser.
2. **Ask AI** — an AI-assisted mode that reads wiki content and answers your question, describes a situation, or drafts a response for you.

The search modal is designed to help you either find the exact clause page quickly, or get a plain-language answer guided by the relevant source pages.

## Opening Search

Click the **Search** bar in the top navigation, or use a keyboard shortcut:

| Action | Shortcut |
|---|---|
| Open search | `Ctrl` + `K` (Windows) or `/` |
| Navigate results | `↑` `↓` arrow keys |
| Open a highlighted result | `Enter` |
| Close search | `Esc` |
| Show keyboard help | `?` |

When the modal opens, you can switch between the **Search** tab and the **Ask AI** tab using the buttons at the top of the modal.

## Search Tab

The **Search** tab is the standard keyword search, powered by Pagefind. It is best when you want to:

- find a specific clause number
- search for a known term such as `overtime`, `allowance`, or `parental leave`
- narrow results by EBA or topic
- browse likely pages quickly before reading

Type a word or phrase into the search box at the top of the modal. Results are ranked by relevance and show:

- the **clause title**
- the **EBA** the page belongs to (shown as a coloured pill)
- a **section / clause breadcrumb**
- a **highlighted excerpt** showing where your search term appears
- any matching **topic tags**

Every clause page also carries search synonyms alongside formal EBA terminology, so everyday language can still lead you to the right page.

| If you search for… | You may find… |
|---|---|
| `sick day`, `off crook` | Personal Leave |
| `time off for a funeral` | Compassionate Leave / Ceremonial Leave |
| `after hours phone calls` | Right to Disconnect |
| `called back to work` | Recall to Duty / Overtime |
| `free meals`, `fed at work` | Meal Allowance |
| `weekend penalty` | Penalty Rates / Saturday / Sunday loadings |
| `study leave`, `course fees` | Professional Development / Education Leave |
| `extra pay for qualifications` | Higher Qualifications Allowance / Top of Band |

### Filters

Below the search box, the Search tab includes two filters:

#### Filter by EBA

Use the **EBA** dropdown to restrict results to one agreement only.

#### Filter by Topic

Use the **Topic** dropdown when you know the subject area but not the clause number. This is especially useful for broad topics such as:

`allowances` · `leave` · `overtime` · `penalty-rates` · `wages` · `termination` · `workload`

#### Using both together

You can combine a keyword, an EBA filter, and a Topic filter. A page must match all active conditions to appear in the results.

::: tip Example
To find wage-related pages for Nurses and Midwives only, open Search, choose **Nurses and Midwives 2024–2028** from the EBA dropdown, then select the **wages** topic or click the **Wage Rates** Quick Access shortcut.
:::

To remove all restrictions, click **Clear filters**.

### Quick Access

When the Search tab opens with no active query and no filters, a **Quick Access** panel appears. This gives you one-click shortcuts into the most common topics:

- ⏱️ **Overtime & Penalty Rates**
- 📅 **Leave Entitlements**
- 💵 **Wage Rates**
- 💰 **Allowances**
- 📋 **Termination & Redundancy**

Clicking one of these shortcuts automatically applies the matching topic filter and runs the search. For example, clicking **Wage Rates** filters results to all pages tagged with the `wages` topic.

### My Bookmarks

If you have bookmarked any clause pages using the bookmark button on a clause page, they will appear in a **My Bookmarks** section at the top of the Quick Access panel. Each bookmark shows the clause title, the EBA, and any note you added when saving it. Clicking a bookmark takes you directly to that clause page.

### Saved Searches

You can save a search query so you can re-run it quickly in future sessions.

While typing a query of two or more characters, a **star icon** appears in the search header. Click it to save the current query, EBA filter, and topic filter together as a saved search. Saved searches appear in the Quick Access panel and persist between browser sessions.

To remove a saved search, click the **×** next to it in the panel, or click the star icon again while the same query is active.

### Recent Searches

The Search tab also shows a **Recent searches** section in the Quick Access panel. This lets you quickly re-run a recent query without typing it again. Recent searches are session-based and clear when you close your browser tab. You can also clear the recent search history from within the modal.

### Result Ranking and Relevance

Search results are ranked so the most relevant pages appear first. Pages closely aligned with the searched topic are surfaced more effectively, meaning:

- exact or close topic matches tend to rank higher
- pages strongly associated with a selected topic are prioritised
- filters help reduce noise when many clauses mention similar words

### Desktop Preview Pane

On a desktop browser, hovering over or focusing on a search result shows a **preview pane** to the right of the modal. This lets you inspect a result before opening it, showing the page title, EBA, breadcrumb, excerpt, and topic tags. The preview pane only appears on larger screens and will not show on mobile devices.

### No Results and Fuzzy Matching

If your search returns no direct matches, the search will automatically try a **similar term**. If a close match is found, results are shown with a "Showing results for **X** instead" note. If no useful results can be found at all, the modal suggests switching to the **Ask AI** tab for a direct answer.

## Ask AI Tab

The **Ask AI** tab is for getting answers from the wiki in plain language. Instead of just listing matching pages, Ask AI reads wiki content and returns a written answer, with links to the source pages it used.

The Ask AI tab has **three modes**, each designed for a different purpose. Select the mode that fits your situation using the three buttons at the top of the Ask AI tab.

---

### Mode 1: Ask a question

Use this mode when you have a question and want a direct answer.

**Fields:**

| Field | Required? | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **EBA** | Optional | Restrict the answer to a specific agreement |
| **Employment type** | Optional | Specify full-time, part-time, casual, or fixed-term |
| **Your question** | Required | The question you want answered |

The **EBA** and **Employment type** dropdowns are optional, but providing them significantly improves the accuracy of the answer. The AI will tailor its response to the specific agreement and employment type you select.

The **Your question** field is a large text box. This is where you type your question. It is designed to allow longer, more detailed questions than a standard search box — the more context you provide, the better the answer.

::: tip Tip
Clicking any of the **example questions** shown at the bottom of the modal will automatically fill the **Your question** field with that example. You can then edit it or submit it as-is.
:::

When you are ready, click **Ask**.

**Example questions:**
- Am I entitled to overtime pay on a public holiday?
- How much notice is required before my roster can be changed?
- What is the recall allowance if I am called back to work after leaving the premises?
- What overtime rates apply after 10 hours on a weekday shift?
- Am I entitled to a meal allowance if I work overtime?

---

### Mode 2: Describe a situation

Use this mode when you are dealing with a real situation — for example, when you are on a call with an employee and need to work out what clause applies to their circumstances.

**Fields:**

| Field | Required? | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **EBA** | Optional | Restrict the answer to a specific agreement |
| **Employment type** | Optional | Specify the employee's employment type |
| **Describe the situation** | Required | A plain-language description of what has happened |

Describe what the employee has told you in your own words. You do not need to know which clause is relevant — that is what the AI will identify for you. The AI will find the most relevant clause, explain what it means, and summarise what the employee may be entitled to.

::: tip Tip
Clicking any of the **example situations** at the bottom of the modal will automatically fill the **Describe the situation** field with that example.
:::

When you are ready, click **Describe situation**.

**Example situations:**
- A casual employee has worked the same regular shifts for over 12 months and wants to convert to part-time.
- An employee was asked to remain at work after their shift ended to cover an absent colleague. They worked an additional 3 hours and are asking what allowances or overtime rates apply.

---

### Mode 3: Draft a response

Use this mode when you have received a question from an employee — for example, through a HR ticket or email — and you want the AI to draft a plain-language reply you can send back to them.

This mode is primarily aimed at HR Advisors.

**Fields:**

| Field | Required? | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **EBA** | Required | The agreement that covers the employee |
| **Employment type** | Required | The employee's employment type |
| **Employee's question** | Required | The question the employee has asked |
| **Additional context** | Optional | Any further details that may help the AI answer accurately |

The **EBA** and **Employment type** fields are mandatory in this mode, because the AI needs to know exactly which agreement and employment type applies in order to draft an accurate response addressed to the employee.

The **Employee's question** field is a single-line input. Enter the question as the employee asked it, or summarise it clearly.

The **Additional context** field is optional, but useful. Use it to provide extra details such as the employee's classification, the day or shift involved, or any other relevant circumstances.

::: tip Tip
Clicking any of the **example employee questions** at the bottom of the modal will automatically fill the **Employee's question** field with that example.
:::

When you are ready, click **Draft response**.

The AI will write a short response (3–5 sentences) addressed directly to the employee, using plain language and citing the relevant clause number.

::: warning Before sending
Review the draft carefully before sending. It is AI-generated and has not been verified by an employment relations specialist. Always check the cited clause and source pages before sending any response to an employee.
:::

**Example employee questions:**
- Am I entitled to overtime pay for the extra hours I worked on the weekend?
- Can my manager change my roster without giving me notice in writing?
- I have run out of sick leave — am I allowed to take unpaid leave instead?
- I have been working the same casual shifts for over a year. Can I convert to part-time?
- What allowance am I entitled to if I am recalled to work after I have already left the premises?

---

### Sources Used

After an answer is returned in any mode, a **Sources used** section appears below the answer. Each source is a hyperlink to the corresponding wiki page. Click a source link to open the clause the answer relied on.

The source pages are the authoritative text — always read the linked clauses in full before acting on any AI-generated answer.

### Following Up

After an answer is returned, you can ask a follow-up question to continue the conversation. The AI will remember the context of the previous exchange (up to the last few turns).

To start a fresh conversation on a new topic, click **New conversation**.

### Important Note About Ask AI

Ask AI is a research aid, not a substitute for reading the clause itself.

Answers are generated from wiki content only. Always verify the answer against the linked source pages and the full EBA text before relying on it for any decision.

## Bookmarking Clause Pages

On any clause page, a **bookmark icon** appears in the toolbar at the top of the page content. Click it to save the page to your bookmarks. You can add a personal note when saving.

Bookmarks are stored in your browser and persist across sessions. They appear in the **My Bookmarks** section of the Quick Access panel whenever you open the Search modal.

## Tips for Better Results

- **Use Search** when you know the term, clause number, or topic.
- **Use Ask a question** when you want a plain-language answer to a specific question.
- **Use Describe a situation** when you are working through a real case and need the AI to identify the relevant clause.
- **Use Draft a response** when you have received a question from an employee and need a ready-to-send reply.
- **Use fewer words** in keyword search — for example, `annual leave cashing out` rather than a full sentence.
- **Use filters early** if you already know the EBA or subject area.
- **Try synonyms** if your first keyword does not return useful results.
- **Use Quick Access** for common categories such as wages, leave, overtime, and allowances.
- **Preview before opening** on desktop if several results look similar.
- **Check the source links** after any Ask AI response.
- **Provide the EBA and employment type** in Ask AI modes whenever possible — it significantly improves answer accuracy.

## Archive Search

The default search does **not** include superseded or expired Enterprise Agreements.

If you need to look up historical entitlements — for example, to interpret something that occurred under a previous agreement — use the dedicated [Archive Search](/eba/archive/search/).
