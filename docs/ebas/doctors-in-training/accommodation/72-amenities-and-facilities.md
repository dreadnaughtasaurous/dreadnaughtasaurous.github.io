# 72. Amenities And Facilities



**72.1** Amenities

The Health Service must provide Doctors access to the following amenities:

**(a)** a changing room with securable storage;

**(b)** a common room; and

**(c)** a shower and bathroom.

**72.2** Facilities

Health Services should provide Doctors access to the following facilities:

**(a)** access to workstations, telecommunication and information technology capable of ensuring administrative and similar work can be accomplished efficiently;

**(b)** access to internet and e-mail facilities for work purposes;

**(c)** 24-hour access to a library and all of its resources;

**(d)** access to a security escort at night;

**(e)** reserved car parking paid for by the Health Service and available for a Doctor when rostered on-call and when recalled to duty. The parking spaces must be well lit and in a secure place within 200 meters from the main entrance of the Health Service; and

**(f)** an office available for private discussion with patients’ relatives.

**72.3** In the case where a Health Service does not meet the standards described in subclause 72.2 above, the Health Service, the Department and the Association will consult to determine a time-frame within which the facilities will be provided within available capital funding budgets.

**72.4** Additional Amenities and Facilities for Doctors working overnight

Where a Doctor is rostered for a period of 12 hours or more commencing after 6.00 p.m., the Health Service must make available to the Doctor for the period of duty:

**(a)** a separate reasonably furnished bedroom with adequate heating and cooling facilities, including a study desk, chair and study light;

**(b)** reasonable provision for the preparation of light refreshments by the Doctor;

**(c)** reasonable provision for laundering, drying and ironing of personal clothing by the Doctor; and

**(d)** rooms fully cleaned and beds made.

Plain language terms: amenities, facilities.
