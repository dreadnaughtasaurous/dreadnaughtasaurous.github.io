# 99. Professional Development Leave

Other than [subclause 99.8](/ebas/mental-health/rpn-pen-mho/education-pd/99-professional-development-leave), this clause does not apply to casual Employees.

**99.1 Professional Development Leave**

- **(a)** All Nurses must meet the continuing professional development (CPD) standards of the Nursing and Midwifery Board of Australia.
- **(b)** CPD means activities that maintain knowledge in the Employee's current area of practice, improve and broaden their knowledge, expertise and competence, and develop the personal and professional qualities required through their professional lives. The CPD cycle involves:
    - **(i)** reviewing practice
    - **(ii)** identifying learning needs
    - **(iii)** planning and participating in relevant learning activities; and
    - **(iv)** reflecting on the value of those activities.



- **(c)** Professional development leave may be utilised:
    - **(i)** to undertake CPD as defined at 99.1(b) and/or
    - **(ii)** to attend nursing or health related conferences, seminars or workshops; and/or
    - **(iii)** for activities including research or home study; and/or
    - **(iv)** to support State Government initiatives to improve workforce development of priority areas of nursing and health care.

---

**99.2 Amount of professional development leave**

- **(a)** **Full-time Employees** are entitled to **five days'** paid professional development leave.
- **(b)** **Part-time Employees** who work a minimum of three shifts per week are entitled to **five days'** paid professional development leave. All other part-time Employees are entitled to **two days** paid professional leave.
- **(c)** An Employee who is a **Nurse Practitioner** will be entitled to a further **10 hours** of paid professional development leave per annum.
- **(d)** Other than in accordance with sub-clause 99.2(e) below, professional development leave does not accumulate from year to year.
- **(e)** Employees may access all or part of a subsequent year’s entitlement during one year. The entitlement for the subsequent year will reduce accordingly.

---

**99.3 Payment** 

A "day" for the purposes of professional development leave is the Employee's **usual shift length**. For example, a night shift worker who takes professional development leave for a 10-hour night shift is entitled to 10 hours payment.

**99.4 Application** 

Professional development leave is available only on **written application**. An Employee must apply to the Employer at least **six weeks** prior to the proposed leave date. If the leave is for home study, the application must detail the relevance of the study to the Employee's employment.

**99.5 Response to application**

- **(a)** Applications will be approved unless there are **exceptional circumstances**.
- **(b)** The Employer must notify the Employee in writing within **seven days**.
- **(c)** If not approved, written reasons must be provided.
- **(d)** The use of this leave is at the **sole discretion of the Employee**, provided the conditions of this clause are met.

---

**99.6 Leave not granted** 

Where a valid application is made but no leave is granted during the calendar year, **one day's leave** will be added to the Employee's accrued annual leave (or handled as mutually agreed).

**99.7 Where leave occurs on a rostered day off** 

If the professional development does not occur on a scheduled work day, the Employer will provide:
- **(a)** A day’s pay at the ordinary rate; or
- **(b)** Time off in lieu (TOIL) within 28 days; or
- **(c)** An additional day’s ordinary pay (if TOIL is not agreed/taken); or
- **(d)** An additional day’s annual leave (no leave loading).

---

**99.8 Mandatory Training**

- **(a)** Any training deemed compulsory by the Employer must occur within **paid time**. Overtime applies where applicable. 
- **(b)** **No deduction** will be made from the Employee's annual professional development leave entitlement for mandatory training.

**99.9 Staff Replacement** 

All absences resulting from approved professional development leave will be **back-filled** in clinical areas where the Employee would ordinarily have a patient allocation.
