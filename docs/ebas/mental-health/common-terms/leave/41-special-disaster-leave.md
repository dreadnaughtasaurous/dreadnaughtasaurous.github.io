# 41. Special Disaster Leave

**41.1** Special disaster leave of up to 3 days per occasion is payable where:

- **(a)** The Employee is a full time or part time employee
- **(b)** Personal Leave is not available either because the Employee has exhausted the accrual or the circumstance does not qualify for Personal Leave; and
- **(c)** the Employee is unable to attend work due to a disaster (such as fire or flood) where:
    - **(i)** the Employee's residence is damaged or under imminent threat of major damage
    - **(ii)** the lives or safety of the Employee or a member of the Employee's immediate family or household is threatened; or
    - **(iii)** there is a formal closure, flooding or other unusual danger of the use of a road(s) which is the Employee's normal travel route to work and no alternative practicable travel route is available.

**41.2** Special disaster leave is non-cumulative.

**41.3** Where requested by the Employer, the Employee must provide evidence that would satisfy a reasonable person of the reasons for accessing leave under this clause.

Plain language terms: disaster leave, emergency leave, flood leave, bushfire leave.
