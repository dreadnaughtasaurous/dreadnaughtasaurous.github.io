# 50. Compassionate Leave

**50.1 Amount of compassionate leave**

- **(a)** Doctors are entitled to **2 days’** compassionate leave on each **permissible occasion** when:
  - **(i)** a member of the Doctor’s immediate family or household:
    - **A.** contracts or develops a personal illness posing a serious threat to life; or
    - **B.** sustains a personal injury posing a serious threat to life; or
    - **C.** dies.
  - **(ii)** a **Stillborn Child** is born, who would have been a member of the Doctor’s immediate family or household.
  - **(iii)** a Doctor, or their spouse/de facto partner, has a **miscarriage** (unless this results in a Stillborn Child).
- **(b)** For illness or injury, the Doctor may take the leave at any time while the condition persists.

---

**50.2 Taking compassionate leave**

- **(a)** Leave may be taken to:
  - **(i)** spend time with the affected family or household member; or
  - **(ii)** attend to matters following the death of the member.
- **(b)** **Non-Accrual:** Unused compassionate leave does not accrue from year to year and is not paid out upon termination.
- **(c)** **Flexibility:** Leave does not have to be taken in consecutive days.
- **(d)** **Unpaid Leave:** A Doctor may take additional unpaid compassionate leave if agreed upon with the Health Service.

---

**50.3 Evidence requirements**

Upon request, a Doctor must provide the Health Service with evidence that would satisfy a **reasonable person** that the leave is being taken for a valid reason. Eligibility for the leave is contingent upon providing this evidence when requested.

Plain language terms: bereavement leave.
