# 49. Personal/Carer's Leave

**49.1 Paid personal/carer’s leave**

- **(a)** Paid personal/carer's Leave is available to a Doctor absent because of:
  - **(i)** personal illness or injury;
  - **(ii)** personal illness or injury of an immediate family or household member;
  - **(iii)** an unexpected emergency affecting an immediate family or household member; or
  - **(iv)** providing ongoing care/attention to a person wholly/substantially dependent on the Doctor (non-commercial basis).
- **(b)** **Basic Entitlement:** Full-time Doctors receive **10 working days** per year (pro rata for Fractional).
- **(c)** **Additional Sick Leave:** Full-time Doctors receive an extra **18 working days** per year specifically for personal illness or injury (pro rata for Fractional).
- **(d)** Entitlements accrue progressively and unused leave accumulates from year to year.

---

**49.2 Immediate family or household**

- **(a)** The term **immediate family** means:
  - **(i)** a spouse, de facto partner, child, parent, grandparent, grandchild, or sibling of the Doctor; or
  - **(ii)** a child, parent, grandparent, grandchild, or sibling of a spouse or de facto partner of the Doctor.

---

**49.3 Use of accumulated personal/carer’s leave**

- **(a) Notification:** Where practicable, the Doctor must notify the Health Service before the start of the shift, providing:
  - **(i)** the reasons for taking leave;
  - **(ii)** the estimated length of absence;
  - **(iii)** (For carer’s leave) the name and relationship of the person requiring care.
- **(b)** If prior notice isn't practical, the Doctor should notify the Health Service by telephone at the first opportunity on the day the absence begins.

---

**49.4 Transfer of unused personal leave accruals**

When transferring between Health Services, accumulated leave (including the additional 18 days) up to a maximum of **280 working days** will be credited to the new employment. Proof (such as a signed statement from the former employer) must be provided by the Doctor.

---

**49.5 Absence on public holidays**

If a public holiday falls during a period of paid personal/carer’s leave, the Doctor is taken **not** to be on personal leave for that day.

---

**49.6 Unpaid personal leave**

If all paid entitlements are exhausted, a Doctor is entitled to unpaid carer’s leave. In the absence of an agreement on the duration, the Doctor is entitled to up to **two days** per occasion.

---

**49.7 Evidence requirements**

Upon request, a Doctor must provide evidence that would satisfy a **reasonable person** that the leave was taken for a valid reason. Leave is not granted unless this evidence is provided upon request.
